Explore Udhampur District

The Gateway to Scenic Beauty and Rich Heritage in Jammu and Kashmir!

Overview of Udhampur

Udhampur, a picturesque town nestled in the foothills of the Trikuta Mountains, is located in the northern Indian union territory of Jammu and Kashmir. Serving as a significant junction between the plains and the mountainous regions, Udhampur is known for its stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and historical significance.Udhampur is a vibrant destination that combines natural beauty with cultural richness, making it an ideal spot for travelers seeking both adventure and tranquility.

Key Attractions

Patnitop

Patnitop is a charming hill station located in the Udhampur district of Jammu and Kashmir, India. Nestled at an altitude of about 2,024 meters (6,637 feet) in the Shivalik range, Patnitop is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, lush green meadows, and serene environment, making it a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

Mansar Lake

Mansar Lake, located about 62 kilometers from Jammu in the Udhampur district of Jammu and Kashmir, is a stunning freshwater lake surrounded by lush greenery and picturesque hills. Renowned for its serene beauty and religious significance, Mansar Lake is a popular destination for both tourists and pilgrims.

Shankri Devta

Shankri Devta is a significant local deity worshipped in the Udhampur district of Jammu and Kashmir. The shrine dedicated to Shankri Devta is situated in the village of Meer, around 15 kilometers from Udhampur town.

Sudh Mahadev

Sudh Mahadev is a sacred temple located in the Udhampur district of Jammu and Kashmir. It is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites in the region, dedicated to Lord Shiva.The temple is believed to be about 2800 years old and holds significant religious importance for Shiva devotees.

Mantalai

Mantalai is a scenic spot located in the Udhampur district of Jammu and Kashmir. It is known for its religious significance and natural beauty. Mantalai is believed to be the site where Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati were married, making it a revered location for Hindu pilgrims.

Ramnagar Fort

Ramnagar Fort, located in Udhampur, Jammu and Kashmir, is an ancient historical fort that holds cultural significance. Built by Raja Suchet Singh in the 19th century, the fort is situated on the banks of the Mansa Devi Nallah (stream), surrounded by scenic landscapes. The architecture is a blend of Mughal and Dogra styles, reflecting the grandeur of that era.

Iskcon Temple Udhampur

ISKCON Temple in Udhampur, Jammu and Kashmir, is a spiritual and cultural center affiliated with the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON). The temple serves as a place of worship and meditation for devotees of Lord Krishna, featuring traditional Hindu architecture and vibrant rituals.

Moungri Cave

Moungri Cave is a significant natural and spiritual site located in the Udhampur district of Jammu and Kashmir. It is a sacred cave that attracts pilgrims, especially during religious festivals. The cave is dedicated to Lord Shiva, and many devotees visit it during the annual Moungri Mela, which is celebrated with much enthusiasm.

Devika Ghats

Devika Ghats, located in Udhampur, Jammu and Kashmir, is a revered site along the banks of the sacred Devika River. Known for its religious significance, the Devika Ghats are often visited by pilgrims who come to take holy dips in the river.

Sanasar

A picturesque and quiet meadow, perfect for nature lovers. Sanasar is often referred to as mini Gulmarg and is famous for adventure sports like paragliding, rock climbing, and trekking.

Kud

A charming small town famous for its scenic beauty and delicious Patisa (a local sweet). Located on the Jammu-Srinagar Highway, it's a great stopover for travelers.

Events & Festivals

Mela Pat

Mela Pat is one of the most popular festivals in Udhampur, celebrated with great enthusiasm for three days. It commemorates the historic meeting between Raja Jagdev Singh and the saint Baba Kaliveer. The mela features folk music, traditional dance performances, and cultural shows.

Navratri Festival

Navratri is a major festival in Jammu dedicated to the worship of the goddess Durga. The city comes alive with vibrant decorations, cultural programs, and religious processions.

Devika Baisakhi Mela

Celebrated along the banks of the sacred Devika River, this mela is a blend of religious and cultural events. Devotees take a holy dip in the Devika River, and the fair includes local crafts, food stalls, and entertainment.

Diwali Festival

Diwali, the festival of lights, is one of the most important festivals in Jammu and across India. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

Holi Festival

Holi, the festival of colors, is celebrated with great enthusiasm in Jammu. It marks the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil. People throw colored powders, spray water, and celebrate by dancing and singing.

Saamba Mela

This local fair is held in honor of Baba Saamba, a revered deity in the region. The fair attracts both pilgrims and tourists, featuring religious offerings and cultural activities.
