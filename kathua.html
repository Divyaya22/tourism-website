Explore Kathua District

Discover the Heritage and Scenic Beauty of Kathua

Overview of Kathua

Kathua, known as the "Gateway to Jammu and Kashmir," is a district rich in cultural heritage and natural beauty. Situated in the southernmost part of the region, it is bordered by Punjab and Himachal Pradesh, making it a strategic and culturally diverse area. Kathua is famous for its historical significance, including ancient temples, forts, and architectural marvels like Jasrota Fort. The district is also a nature lover’s paradise, with picturesque landscapes, serene rivers like the Ujh, and lush green forests that offer a peaceful retreat. The blend of tradition, spirituality, and scenic beauty makes Kathua a unique destination for tourists.

Key Attractions

Sukrala mata temple

Sukrala Mata Temple, located in the Billawar area of Kathua district, is a revered Hindu shrine dedicated to Goddess Sukrala Mata, an incarnation of Goddess Durga. Perched atop a hill, the temple is surrounded by scenic landscapes, offering a peaceful and spiritual retreat for devotees and visitors. The temple holds significant religious importance and is believed to grant wishes and bring prosperity.

Bala sundari mandir

Bala Sundari Mandir, located near Billawar in the Kathua district, is a prominent temple dedicated to Goddess Bala Sundari, a form of Goddess Durga. This ancient temple is deeply revered by locals and pilgrims who come to seek blessings for health, prosperity, and protection. Nestled amidst picturesque surroundings, the temple offers a peaceful and spiritual atmosphere.

Airwan temple

Airwan Temple, located in Kathua district, is a sacred shrine known for its spiritual significance and historical relevance. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, the temple is an important pilgrimage site for devotees in the region. The temple’s tranquil location, amidst the lush greenery and calm surroundings, makes it a peaceful place for worship and meditation.

Mata Chanchlo devi temple

Mata Chanchlo Devi Temple, located near Basohli in Kathua district, is a revered shrine dedicated to Goddess Chanchlo Devi, believed to be an incarnation of Goddess Durga. The temple is situated on a hilltop, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape, making the journey to the temple both spiritually and visually rewarding.

Purthu Beach

Purthu Beach, located near Basohli in Kathua district, is a scenic spot on the banks of the Ranjit Sagar Lake. Known for its serene ambiance and natural beauty, the beach has become a popular destination for tourists looking to relax by the water or enjoy activities such as boating and picnicking. The calm waters and surrounding hills make it a picturesque spot for photography and unwinding in nature.

Ranjit sagar dam

Ranjit Sagar Dam, located near the border of Punjab and Kathua district in Jammu and Kashmir, is one of the largest dams in northern India. Built on the Ravi River, it is a major hydroelectric project that not only supports irrigation and power generation but has also become a popular tourist attraction.

Atal Setu

Atal Setu, a cable-stayed bridge over the Ravi River, connects Kathua to the neighboring state of Punjab. The bridge is not only a major infrastructural landmark but also a tourist attraction due to its impressive design and the beautiful views of the river and surrounding landscape.

Jasrota Fort Temple

Jasrota Fort is a historical monument built during the rule of the Jasrotia kings. The fort, surrounded by dense forests, offers a glimpse into the architectural brilliance of its time. Though now in ruins, the fort still attracts history enthusiasts and nature lovers alike for its scenic views and serene environment.

Ujh Barrage

Ujh Barrage is a popular picnic spot located on the Ujh River. Surrounded by lush greenery, it is an excellent place to relax and enjoy the peaceful surroundings. The area is also a hotspot for birdwatchers, as migratory birds frequent the region during certain seasons.

Events & Festivals

Baisakhi

Baisakhi marks the harvest festival and the Punjabi New Year. It is celebrated with traditional music, dance, and feasting. In Kathua, local communities come together to celebrate with folk dances like Bhangra and Gidda, showcasing their rich cultural heritage.

Gurpurab

Gurpurab, commemorating the birth of Guru Nanak Dev Ji, is celebrated by the Sikh community in Kathua. The festival includes processions, singing of hymns, and community meals (Langar) at gurdwaras, promoting the values of equality and community service.

Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha

These Islamic festivals are celebrated with prayers, feasting, and community gatherings. Special prayers are held in mosques, and families share meals and gifts.

Diwali Festival

Diwali, the festival of lights, is one of the most important festivals across India. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

Holi Festival

Holi, the festival of colors, is celebrated with great enthusiasm in India. It marks the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil. People throw colored powders, spray water, and celebrate by dancing and singing.

Navratri Festival

Navratri is a major festival celebrated in reasi dedicated to the worship of the goddess Durga. The city comes alive with vibrant decorations, cultural programs, and religious processions.
