Explore Kishtwar District

The Land of Sapphire Valleys and Majestic Peaks

Overview of Kishtwar

Kishtwar is a picturesque district located in the Doda region of Jammu and Kashmir, India. Nestled in the majestic Himalayas, it is known for its stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and diverse natural beauty.Kishtwar is situated at an elevation of about 1,600 meters (5,250 feet) above sea level, surrounded by lush green valleys, dense forests, and snow-capped mountains.he district is blessed with numerous rivers, including the Chenab River, which flows through the region, adding to its scenic beauty and providing opportunities for water sports and fishing.

Key Attractions

Chowgan Kishtwar

Chowgan is a large and picturesque meadow located in the Kishtwar district of Jammu and Kashmir, known for its natural beauty. It's an open field surrounded by lush greenery and scenic views of the surrounding hills and forests, often used by locals and visitors for recreation and various cultural or sporting activities.

Machail Mata

Machail Mata is a revered shrine dedicated to Goddess Chandi, located in the Machail village of the Kishtwar district in Jammu and Kashmir. The temple attracts thousands of devotees every year, especially during the annual Machail Yatra, which takes place in August.

Kishtwar National Park

Kishtwar National Park is a pristine and beautiful national park located in the Kishtwar district of Jammu and Kashmir. Established in 1981, the park covers an area of around 400 square kilometers and is known for its rich biodiversity, stunning landscapes, and snow-capped mountains. It is situated at altitudes ranging from 1,700 to 4,800 meters, making it a haven for various flora and fauna, some of which are rare and endangered.

Sinthan Top

Sinthan Top is a stunning mountain pass located between the Kishtwar and Anantnag districts of Jammu and Kashmir. Situated at an elevation of around 3,748 meters (12,300 feet), Sinthan Top is known for its breathtaking views, snow-capped peaks, and panoramic vistas of the surrounding valleys. It serves as a link between the Kashmir Valley and the Jammu region.

Bimal Nag

Bimal Nag is a serene and beautiful high-altitude lake located in the Kishtwar district of Jammu and Kashmir. It is surrounded by lush green meadows, dense forests, and majestic mountains, offering a peaceful retreat for nature lovers and trekkers. The area around Bimal Nag is relatively untouched by commercialization, making it an ideal destination for those seeking tranquility and natural beauty.

Mughal Maidan

Mughal Maidan is a picturesque valley located in the Kishtwar district of Jammu and Kashmir. It is known for its historical significance, as it is believed that the Mughal emperors, particularly Emperor Akbar, used this route during their expeditions to Kashmir. The place is surrounded by lush green meadows, rolling hills, and scenic landscapes, making it an attractive spot for visitors.

Hanswar Waterfall

Hanswar Waterfall is a hidden gem located in the Kishtwar district of Jammu and Kashmir. This lesser-known waterfall is surrounded by lush green forests and rugged terrain, offering a peaceful and refreshing natural setting. The beauty of the cascading water, combined with the serene and relatively untouched environment, makes Hanswar Waterfall a great spot for nature lovers and adventurers.

Daksum Waterfall

Daksum Waterfall is a scenic waterfall located in the Daksum region, nestled within the Kokernag area of Anantnag district, Jammu and Kashmir. It is surrounded by lush green forests, rolling meadows, and snow-clad mountains, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers and trekkers. The waterfall is a major attraction for visitors coming to Daksum, a region renowned for its natural beauty, tranquil surroundings, and pristine landscapes.

Devigoal picnic spot

Devigoal Picnic Spot is a beautiful and serene location situated in the Kishtwar district of Jammu and Kashmir. Known for its tranquil atmosphere, lush greenery, and picturesque surroundings, it serves as an ideal getaway for locals and tourists alike who want to enjoy a peaceful day in nature.

Events & Festivals

Local Fairs and Melas

Various local fairs are held throughout the year, showcasing local crafts, foods, and cultural performances, providing a platform for community bonding.

Machail Mata Yatra

This annual pilgrimage to the Machail Mata temple attracts thousands of devotees. The yatra usually occurs in August and involves a trek through beautiful landscapes.

Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha

These Islamic festivals are celebrated with prayers, feasting, and community gatherings. Special prayers are held in mosques, and families share meals and gifts.

Diwali Festival

Diwali, the festival of lights, is one of the most important festivals in Jammu and across India. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

Holi Festival

Holi, the festival of colors, is celebrated with great enthusiasm in Jammu. It marks the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil. People throw colored powders, spray water, and celebrate by dancing and singing.

Navratri Festival

Navratri is a major festival celebrated in reasi dedicated to the worship of the goddess Durga. The city comes alive with vibrant decorations, cultural programs, and religious processions.
