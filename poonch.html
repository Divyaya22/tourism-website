Poonch District

Where Nature Meets Heritage

Overview of Poonch

Poonch District, nestled in the Pir Panjal range of Jammu and Kashmir, is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes and rich cultural heritage. Spanning approximately 1,700 square kilometers, this region is characterized by lush green valleys, pristine rivers, and stunning mountains, making it a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.The district is home to diverse flora and fauna, offering opportunities for trekking, hiking, and wildlife observation. Poonch's rich history is reflected in its ancient temples, mosques, and fort ruins, which showcase a blend of various cultural influences.

Key Attractions

Noori Chamb

Noori Chamb is a beautiful waterfall located in the Poonch district of Jammu and Kashmir. It is a popular tourist spot, known for its scenic beauty and serene surroundings. The waterfall is nestled amidst lush greenery, offering a tranquil escape for visitors.

Peer Ki Gali

Peer Ki Gali is a stunning mountain pass located in the Poonch district of Jammu and Kashmir. It is a key highlight of the Mughal Road, which connects the Poonch and Rajouri districts to the Kashmir Valley.

Poonch Fort

Poonch Fort, located in the Poonch district of Jammu and Kashmir, is a significant historical and architectural marvel. Built by Raja Rustam Khan in the 18th century, the fort reflects Mughal architectural influence.

Nandishool

Nandishool is a scenic waterfall located in the Poonch district of Jammu and Kashmir. It is surrounded by lush greenery and offers a peaceful environment for visitors. The waterfall is considered a hidden gem in the region

Mandi

Mandi is a scenic and culturally rich town located in the Poonch district of Jammu and Kashmir. It's known for its natural beauty, historical significance, and spiritual sites.

Than Pir

Than Pir is a scenic and serene location in the Poonch district of Jammu and Kashmir, known for its natural beauty and religious significance. The area is set against the picturesque backdrop of the Pir Panjal mountain range, offering stunning views and a peaceful environment.

Surankote

Surankote is a town in the Poonch district of Jammu and Kashmir, located in the Pir Panjal Range. It's known for its scenic beauty, with lush green meadows, hills, and streams, making it a place of interest for nature lovers and adventurers.

Navgrah Temple

The Navgrah Temple in Poonch district is an important religious site dedicated to the nine celestial bodies (Navgrah), which hold significance in Hindu astrology.

Loran

Loran is a small town located in the Poonch district of Jammu and Kashmir, India. It's known for its picturesque landscapes and serene environment, making it a charming destination for visitors.

Events & Festivals

Navratri Festival

Navratri is a major festival celebrated in reasi dedicated to the worship of the goddess Durga. The city comes alive with vibrant decorations, cultural programs, and religious processions.

Diwali Festival

Diwali, the festival of lights, is one of the most important festivals in reasi and across India. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

Holi Festival

Holi, the festival of colors, is celebrated with great enthusiasm in reasi. It marks the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil. People throw colored powders, spray water, and celebrate by dancing and singing.

Shivratri

Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this festival involves night-long prayers, fasting, and various religious rituals. Temples are beautifully decorated, and devotees participate in special prayers.

Ramzan

The holy month of fasting is observed with devotion, and the end of Ramzan is marked by the grand celebration of Eid-ul-Fitr.
