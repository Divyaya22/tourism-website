Explore Rajouri District

Discover the Enchantment of Rajouri: Nature, Culture, and Adventure Await!

Overview of Rajouri

Rajouri, nestled in the Pir Panjal range of the Himalayas, is a district in the Indian Union Territory of Jammu and Kashmir. Rich in history and natural beauty, it boasts a diverse landscape that includes lush valleys, rolling hills, and dense forests.Historically significant, Rajouri has witnessed the rise and fall of various empires and is home to ancient temples and historical sites, such as the stunning Akhnoor Fort and the revered Baba Ghulam Shah Badshah Shrine.

Key Attractions

Baba Ghulam Shah badshah shrine

This revered shrine is dedicated to Baba Ghulam Shah Badshah, a Sufi saint, and is a significant pilgrimage site for locals and visitors alike. The tranquil surroundings and beautiful architecture make it a serene place for reflection and worship.

Dhanidhar Fort

Built in the 18th century, this historical fort showcases the architectural brilliance of its time. Located on a hilltop, it offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. The fort is steeped in history and serves as a reminder of Rajouri's rich past.

Nowshera

A small town located about 30 km from Rajouri, Nowshera is known for its lush greenery and picturesque landscapes. It is an ideal spot for picnics and leisurely walks amidst nature.

Chani Prat Temple

The Chani Prat Temple is a revered spiritual site located in the Rajouri district of Jammu and Kashmir. Nestled amidst scenic landscapes, this temple holds religious and cultural significance for the local population. It's dedicated to Hindu deities, and the area around it is known for serene natural beauty, offering visitors a peaceful environment for meditation and worship.

Darhal malkan

Darhal Malkan is a picturesque village located in the Rajouri district of Jammu and Kashmir. It is known for its natural beauty, with lush green landscapes and rolling hills, making it a serene and peaceful spot. The region around Darhal Malkan is surrounded by mountains and forests, offering visitors an immersive experience in nature.

Sunderbani

Sunderbani is a small town located in the Rajouri district of Jammu and Kashmir. Known for its peaceful environment and picturesque surroundings, it lies along the Jammu-Rajouri-Poonch highway, making it easily accessible. The town is surrounded by lush green hills and scenic landscapes typical of the region, offering a serene retreat for travelers and locals alike.

Deera Ki Gali

Deera Ki Gali (often abbreviated as DKG) is a well-known mountain pass situated between the Rajouri and Poonch districts of Jammu and Kashmir. It lies along the historic Mughal Road, which is a significant route connecting the Kashmir Valley with the rest of the state, particularly the districts of Rajouri and Poonch.

Bhag sar

Bhag Sar Lake is a beautiful and serene high-altitude lake located in the Rajouri district of Jammu and Kashmir. Situated amidst the majestic Pir Panjal mountain range, this lake is a hidden gem for nature lovers and trekking enthusiasts.

Chandan sar lake

Chandan Sar Lake is a pristine, high-altitude lake located in the Pir Panjal range within the Rajouri district of Jammu and Kashmir. Known for its striking beauty, the lake is surrounded by lush green meadows, rocky terrains, and towering snow-clad mountains. The crystal-clear waters of Chandan Sar reflect the blue sky and the surrounding natural beauty, creating a serene and picturesque environment.

Events & Festivals

Shivratri

Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this festival involves night-long prayers, fasting, and various religious rituals. Temples are beautifully decorated, and devotees participate in special prayers.

Lohri Festival

Lohri, a vibrant and joyful festival celebrated in Jammu during the winter season, brings together people for bonfires, songs, and dance to mark the end of winter and welcome the longer days.

Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha

These Islamic festivals are celebrated with prayers, feasting, and community gatherings. Special prayers are held in mosques, and families share meals and gifts.

Diwali Festival

Diwali, the festival of lights, is one of the most important festivals across India. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

Holi Festival

Holi, the festival of colors, is celebrated with great enthusiasm in India. It marks the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil. People throw colored powders, spray water, and celebrate by dancing and singing.

Navratri Festival

Navratri is a major festival celebrated in reasi dedicated to the worship of the goddess Durga. The city comes alive with vibrant decorations, cultural programs, and religious processions.
