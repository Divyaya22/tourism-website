Explore Jammu District

The City of Temples and Gateway to Heaven

Overview of Jammu

Tourism is the largest industry in Jammu city. It is also a focal point for the pilgrims going to Vaishno Devi and Kashmir valley as it is second last railway terminal in North India. All the routes leading to Kashmir, Poonch, Doda and Laddakh start from Jammu city. So throughout the year, the city remains full of people from all the parts of India. Places of interest include old historic palaces like Mubarak Mandi Palace, Purani Mandi, Rani Park, Amar Mahal, Bahu Fort, Raghunath Temple, Ranbireshwar Temple, Karbala, Peer Meetha, Old city.

Key Attractions

Raghunath Temple

A grand temple dedicated to Lord Rama, built in the 19th century.

Bahu Fort & Gardens

An ancient fort overlooking the Tawi River, with beautiful Mughal gardens.

Mubarak Mandi Palace

The royal residence of the Dogra dynasty, this palace is a fine example of Dogra, Rajasthani, and European architecture.

Amar Mahal Palace

A beautiful palace-turned-museum, showcasing the heritage of the royal family of Jammu and various historical artifacts.

Peer Kho Cave Temple

A temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in a cave with a serene ambiance and religious significance.

Ranbireshwar Temple

Another major temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, featuring one of the largest Shiva Lingams in Northern India.

Nandini Wildlife Sanctuary

Located near Jammu, this sanctuary is a natural habitat for various species of wildlife and a perfect spot for nature lovers.

Purmandal Temple

Often referred to as 'Chhota Kashi,' Purmandal is an ancient temple town dedicated to Lord Shiva, located on the banks of the Devika River.

Akhnoor Fort

Located on the banks of the Chenab River, this historic fort has both Mughal and Sikh era architecture and offers beautiful views of the river and the surrounding landscape.

Panchvaktra Temple

One of the oldest temples in Jammu, dedicated to Lord Shiva. The name Panchvaktra refers to the five-faced image of Lord Shiva in this temple.

Mahamaya Temple

This temple is dedicated to Goddess Mahamaya, a local deity, and is located on the banks of the Tawi River, with scenic views of the surrounding area.

Gole Market

A historic marketplace that represents the traditional bazaars of Jammu, where tourists can shop for local handicrafts, textiles, and souvenirs.

Wave mall

A popular shopping destination in Jammu, offering a variety of retail outlets, entertainment options, and a multiplex cinema, making it a hub for both locals and tourists.

Palm Island mall

Palm Island Mall in Jammu is a popular shopping and entertainment destination offering a wide variety of retail outlets, restaurants, and recreational activities for visitors.

Tirupati Balaji Temple

A beautiful replica of the famous Tirupati Balaji Temple in Andhra Pradesh, this temple in Jammu is a significant religious site for devotees, offering serene surroundings and spiritual solace.

Hunter's Ranch

A popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts, Hunter's Ranch offers a unique blend of leisure and outdoor activities, including horseback riding, trekking, and a chance to experience the natural beauty of the Jammu region.

Events & Festivals

Lohri Festival

Lohri, a vibrant and joyful festival celebrated in Jammu during the winter season, brings together people for bonfires, songs, and dance to mark the end of winter and welcome the longer days.

Navratri Festival

Navratri is a major festival in Jammu dedicated to the worship of the goddess Durga. The city comes alive with vibrant decorations, cultural programs, and religious processions.

Baisakhi Festival

Baisakhi is celebrated in Jammu with colorful processions and traditional music, marking the harvest season and the Punjabi New Year. It showcases the region’s agrarian culture.

Diwali Festival

Diwali, the festival of lights, is one of the most important festivals in Jammu and across India. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

Holi Festival

Holi, the festival of colors, is celebrated with great enthusiasm in Jammu. It marks the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil. People throw colored powders, spray water, and celebrate by dancing and singing.

Maharaja Hari Singh Birthday

Maharaja Hari Singh, the last ruling Maharaja of Jammu and Kashmir, is celebrated with great respect and admiration across Jammu on his birth anniversary, which falls on September 23.
