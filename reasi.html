Reasi District

Gateway to Adventure and Spiritual Serenity

Overview of Reasi

Reasi is a picturesque district in the Jammu region of Jammu and Kashmir, known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic landscapes, and religious significance. It is home to the famous Vaishno Devi Shrine, one of the most visited pilgrimage sites in India, attracting millions of devotees annually. Reasi is also a hub for adventure tourism, with opportunities for trekking, rock climbing, and water sports at places like the Chenab River and Bhimgarh Fort. The district's natural beauty is further enhanced by its lush valleys, waterfalls, and serene surroundings, making it a perfect destination for both spiritual seekers and nature enthusiasts.

Key Attractions

Siar Baba Waterfall

Siar Baba Waterfall in Reasi cascades down a towering cliff, offering a breathtaking natural retreat and a popular spot for adventure seekers and picnickers alike.

Shiv khori

Shiv Khori, nestled in Reasi, is a revered cave shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, attracting pilgrims with its natural formation of the Shivalinga and mystical aura.

Shri Mata Vaishno Devi Shrine

Shri Mata Vaishno Devi Shrine, nestled in the Trikuta Mountains of Reasi, is one of India's most revered pilgrimage sites, drawing millions of devotees seeking divine blessings.

Bhimgarh Fort

Bhimgarh Fort, perched on a hilltop in Reasi, offers a glimpse into the region's rich history and panoramic views of the surrounding landscapes.

Salal Power Project

The Salal Power Project in Reasi is a major hydroelectric venture harnessing the Chenab River's energy, contributing significantly to the region's power supply.

Baba Aghar Jitto

Baba Aghar Jitto in Reasi is a revered shrine dedicated to a legendary farmer-saint, symbolizing the spirit of sacrifice and devotion.

Nau Devi Mata Mandir

Nau Devi Mata Mandir in Reasi is a revered shrine nestled within a cave, where devotees worship the nine forms of Goddess Durga in a tranquil and spiritual atmosphere.

Devi Pindi

Devi Pindi in Reasi is a serene and lesser-known pilgrimage site, offering a peaceful retreat for devotees amidst nature's beauty.

Jhajjar Kotli

Jhajjar Kotli: A Serene Riverside Retreat and Adventure Hub in Reasi District.

Railway Bridge

The Railway Bridge in Reasi is a significant infrastructure project that forms part of the Udhampur-Srinagar-Baramulla Rail Link (USBRL) project. This bridge is notable for its impressive engineering, as it spans the Chenab River and connects various regions

Events & Festivals

Navratri Festival

Navratri is a major festival celebrated in reasi dedicated to the worship of the goddess Durga. The city comes alive with vibrant decorations, cultural programs, and religious processions.

Diwali Festival

Diwali, the festival of lights, is one of the most important festivals in reasi and across India. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.

Holi Festival

Holi, the festival of colors, is celebrated with great enthusiasm in reasi. It marks the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil. People throw colored powders, spray water, and celebrate by dancing and singing.

Vaishno Devi Yatra

Pilgrimage season peaks during the festival months, attracting millions of devotees who walk the sacred route to the Vaishno Devi Temple.
