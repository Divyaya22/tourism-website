Srinagar District

Explore the Beauty of Srinagar: The Jewel of Kashmir

Overview of Srinagar

Srinagar, the summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir, is renowned for its stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Nestled amidst the majestic Himalayas, the city is famous for its picturesque Dal Lake, where houseboats and shikaras (traditional wooden boats) create a serene ambiance. With vibrant Mughal gardens, historic temples, and lush landscapes, Srinagar offers a unique blend of nature and culture. Visitors can explore the bustling markets filled with local handicrafts, savor the flavors of Kashmiri cuisine, and immerse themselves in the warmth of its hospitable people. Whether you’re seeking adventure or tranquility, Srinagar is a destination that leaves a lasting impression.

Key Attractions

Dal Lake

Dal Lake, often called the "Jewel in the crown of Kashmir," is a serene and breathtaking water body in Srinagar, famous for its houseboats, shikaras, and floating gardens.

Shalimar Bagh

Shalimar Bagh, a stunning Mughal garden in Srinagar, is a masterpiece of Persian-inspired architecture, known for its terraced lawns, elegant fountains, and vibrant flowerbeds.

Nishat Bagh

Nishat Bagh, a stunning Mughal garden overlooking Dal Lake in Srinagar, is famed for its terraced lawns, vibrant flower beds, and breathtaking views of the Zabarwan Mountains.

Chashme Shahi Garden

Chashme Shahi Garden, one of the famous Mughal gardens in Srinagar, is known for its pristine natural spring and beautifully terraced lawns, offering a peaceful retreat with breathtaking views.

Shankaracharya Temple

The Shankaracharya Temple, perched atop the Shankaracharya Hill in Srinagar, offers breathtaking panoramic views of the city and Dal Lake.

Pari Mahal

Pari Mahal, also known as the "Palace of Fairies," is a stunning terraced garden in Srinagar offering panoramic views of Dal Lake and the surrounding mountains.

Kheer Bhawani Temple

The Kheer Bhawani Temple, nestled in the village of Tulmul near Srinagar, is a revered Hindu shrine dedicated to the goddess Ragnya Devi, known for its sacred spring that changes color, reflecting the mystical energy of the site.

Tulip Garden

The Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden in Srinagar, the largest in Asia, blooms with vibrant colors each spring, offering a breathtaking view of over a million tulips set against the backdrop of the Zabarwan Range.

Gulmarg

Gulmarg literally means “Meadow of flowers”. Gulmarg is located 60 KMs away from Srinagar which is an hour and half journey by car.

Sonmarg

Sonamarg had historical significance as a gateway on the ancient Silk Road, connecting Kashmir with Tibet. Today, the hill station is a popular tourist destination amongst fishers and hikers

Pahalgam

Pahalgam is a popular tourist destination and hill station. Its lush green meadows and pristine waters attract thousands of tourists from all over the world each year.

Lal Chowk

Lal Chowk is a city square in Srinagar, in the Indian union territory of Jammu and Kashmir.It houses a clock tower.It has evolved into Srinagar's main business district

Doodhpathri

Doodhpathri is a tourist destination and a hill station located in the Khansahib tehsil of the Budgam district of Jammu and Kashmir, India.

Wular Lake

Wular Lake , is one of the largest fresh water lakes in South Asia. The lake basin was formed as a result of tectonic activity and is fed by the Jhelum River and stream Madhumati and Arin.

Events & Festivals

Navroz

Celebrated by the Shia Muslim community, Navroz marks the Persian New Year and is an important occasion in Srinagar, observed with feasts, prayers, and social gatherings.

Tulip Festival

Held in April at the Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden, this festival celebrates the blooming of thousands of tulips, attracting tourists from all over the world.

Shikara Festival

Organized in July-August by the Jammu and Kashmir Tourism Department, this festival celebrates Srinagar’s iconic Shikara boats. It includes boat races, cultural performances, and a showcase of traditional Kashmiri crafts.

Shivratri

Celebrated by Kashmiri Pandits in Srinagar, this festival is observed with prayers and rituals, especially devoted to Lord Shiva.

Kashmir Saffron Festival

Celebrated in Pampore near Srinagar, this festival showcases the saffron harvest in October-November, allowing visitors to witness the cultivation and processing of Kashmir’s famous saffron.

Ramzan

The holy month of fasting is observed with devotion, and the end of Ramzan is marked by the grand celebration of Eid-ul-Fitr.
